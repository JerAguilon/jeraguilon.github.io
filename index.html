About Me

Currently I'm a third year in Georgia Tech's Computer Science program, set to graduate December 2018. I self-taught myself Java out of curiosity in high school and have been hooked since.

If I had to pick my favorite personal project, it's hands down my Icon-Based Asthma Action Plan, which I did for research out in Tech Square. As somebody who was constantly troubled by the disease, I find value in knowing that my work could measurably improve the lives of others around me.

I have a lot of uncommon interests, but my best "party trick" is the fact that by the time you finish reading this sentence out loud, I could have solved an entire Rubik's Cube.

Software Engineering Intern
Personalization, Summer 2017

Personalization deals with recommending new products and services to customers. Naturally, one of the biggest challenges with cataloging Amazon's huge array of products is latency. In order to incorporate more complex (and by extension more relevant) algorithms, there is a tradeoff in computational cost. In order to include this more sophisticated model in widgets with low latency requirements, I created a NoSQL solution with preprocessed recommendation scores for over 100 million listings.

Software Engineering Intern
Cloud Monitoring, Spring 2017

As a member of cloud monitoring team, I improved usability of the analytics pipelines through a new pipeline monitoring service. This service aggregates the statuses of the pipelines and traces dependencies between the individual components. By storing pipeline statuses in a NewSQL database, I made complex tasks such as diagnosing the root cause of a cascading pipeline automatic. My entrance into this program was quite unique, as I got my interview after completing the Google Foobar challenge. Check out my portfolio for what that is!

Software Engineering Intern
Backend & Test, Summer 2016

In summer 2016, I had the opportunity to intern at Vertafore for the purpose of aiding them in developing insurance software to facilitate productivity. I helped integrate an array of cloud storage management solutions that are being implemented across all of the company's products. I also contributed to the initiative of providing high quality unit and end-to-end tests across the entire platform.

UROC Undergraduate Researcher
Full stack developer, Winter 2016

Asthma is a chronic condition that afflicts at a higher rate for those of low socioeconomic status. One of the most effective ways for parents to manage their childrens' asthma is through an Asthma Action Plan. Research has shown that icon-based plans have improved the health care management of those with lower reading comprehension. My role under Dr. Rosa Arriaga was to implement the backend of the IBAAP with the front end prototype.

College of Computing Outreach Program
Middle School Teacher

The initiative of the Outreach Program at Georgia Tech is to facilitate computational learning in disadvantaged areas in Atlanta. As a tutor, I got to introduce middle schoolers to functional programming, a term I had never even heard of at their age. By working weekly with these kids, I really got to understand how accessible, enjoyable, and fulfilling programming can be for people of all walks of life.

Portfolio

IBAAP [link]

An icon-based, mobile-oriented asthma action plan that I developed as part of research under Dr. Arriaga. The goal is to aid disadvantaged families by making these plans easily understandable. Has full CRUD functionalities in order to track statistics of childrens' health.

Google Foobar [link]

A collection of my solutions, thought processes, and tests for the hacker challenges I did under a secretive recruiting tool given by Google. Topics include computational challenges behind cryptographic hashing, graph theory, counting, and complexity. Completing this got me an interview and eventual offer for a Winter 2016 internship!

The Clean Water Project (Java) [link]

A group project for CS2340, Georgia Tech's Object Oriented Design class. 783 million people live without steady access to water. This project aims to provide a means of tracking where clean water is available. We follow a crowd sourced approach, allowing users to enter where clean water has been found. Moreover, managers can track the cleanliness of water in particular locations over time.

The Clean Water Project (MEAN stack) [link]

Although the scope of CS2340 only required a Java implementation, I created a RESTful API in node.js to simplify server-side operations. I'm particularly proud of how just about all business logic is server-sided, meaning we never trust user input. Naturally, this lead to an entire MEAN stack implementation of the app.

Spray and Pray [link]

A practice tool for the popular game Counter-Strike: Global Offensive, the most competitive shooter in the e-Sports industry and a personal pastime of mine. Released on Reddit to 500 upvoting users who can now practice on the go without needing a powerful computer.
